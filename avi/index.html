A Boy From Varanasi

(He writes because he forgets.)

Last Updated: March 2026
Age:

This is not for LinkedIn.
This is for reference.
Because I forget how things actually felt.

1. Where I Started

Varanasi

I was born on 4 December 1996 in Varanasi.

Varanasi is slow. Very slow. It teaches patience without saying it.

My father had a shop near BHU Trauma Center. I used to sit there sometimes. I saw people waiting outside the hospital. I saw worried faces. I saw families fully dependent on doctors.

I didn’t understand much at that time. But I think I learned something silently:
Life can change anytime. Stability is not guaranteed.

Maybe that is why I want stability so badly today.

2. Class 10

The Stable Happiness

Sunbeam Academy, Varanasi.

This was the best time. We were not ambitious. We were busy.

School → Coaching → Cricket → PS2 → Repeat.

We had fixed friends. Fixed routine. Fixed life.

Now I understand something important:
We were happy because of pattern. Same people. Same system. No uncertainty.

Lesson to remember:
Stability makes me calm. Routine makes me happy.

3. Class 11 & 12

The Break

Everything changed. School changed. Friends changed. I got suspended. I moved to KV BHU.

Those two years were heavy. Board exams in India are not normal exams. They are generational pressure. My grandfather gave them. My father gave them. Then I gave them.

I saw parents emotionally dependent on children’s marks. I didn’t like that idea. I asked myself:
How can you depend fully on someone else for your happiness?

At the same time, I also felt pressure. I passed with good marks.

But the bigger lesson was this:
Dependency is risky.
Pressure builds you if you survive it.
I survived it.

4. College (BCA)

University of Lucknow

College was better. More free. Less pressure. Only 11th and 12th were really hard phases in my life. Rest has been manageable.

Final year project: Prixfit
Idea: Farmers don’t know where they get better crop prices. So build a system that shows mandi prices and helps them decide.

I still like that thinking. I always liked solving real problems.

What college gave me? Honestly, no powerful network. No strong reference. I never had big influential people around me. Everything I built, I built mostly alone.

Sometimes I feel proud of that. Sometimes I feel maybe having strong network could have made things faster.

But reality: I built without dependency.

5. Startup

2019 to 2022

After graduation, I started my own startup.

It was for visually impaired students. We wanted to give them seamless learning because they don’t have easy access to books.

Then COVID came. Money was low. Hope was low. Exchange program cancelled. No clarity about future.

We survived. Somehow.

Important things I learned:
Building from scratch is extremely hard.
Team handling is not easy.
Money flow is oxygen.
Motivation without growth dies.
Support from people matters a lot.

Here I chased money a lot. Because survival mode. But I also felt proud. We didn’t quit immediately.

At some point, I realised the startup was not scaling. It was becoming like a job without growth. So I had to choose stability.

Lesson: Dreams are good. But system and scale matter more.

6. Smartivity

Learning Business Reality (2022)

I joined Smartivity as Product Manager.

It was a small company. Smaller than my startup. But they were generating revenue. That itself was learning.

I revamped the website. Worked on CRO. Understood funnels. Studied D2C vs Amazon.

Observation: Amazon performs better than D2C in many cases because trust is already built.

I also connected with investors and understood how they think.

Hard part? People. Some people avoid responsibility. Some delay work to protect their job. Some carry entire revenue on their back.

I learned:
Data-driven execution is powerful.
People management is more complex than product building.

I left because company future didn’t look stable.

Lesson: Choose trajectory, not attachment.

7. Current

Mahindra

Now I work at Mahindra as Product Manager.

Bigger organisation. More structure. More stakeholders.

Earlier I built in chaos. Now I build inside system. Both teach different things.

Right now I am focusing on learning scale and long-term thinking.

8. Physical Reset

Discipline Works

Three years ago I was overweight.

Now I am around 82 kg. Target below 70.
I walk around 9 km daily.
I quit sugar.

This is not about looks. This is proof that I can redesign myself.

Discipline works.

9. Internal Struggles

Improving Slowly

I struggle with speaking in front of CEO-level people.
Sometimes I just read instead of presenting confidently.
I struggle with writing clearly even when I know the idea.
I overthink sometimes.
I want stability too much.

But I am improving. Slowly.

10. My Ambition

(Which I Don’t Say Loudly)

One day, I will build again.

But this time:
With better system.
With capital awareness.
With distribution strategy.
With long-term thinking.

I don’t want to just manage features.
I want to build platforms.

11. Why I Am Writing This

Because I forget.

I forget how hard 11th was.
I forget how scary COVID felt.
I forget how proud I felt after losing weight.
I forget how much effort it took to reach here without big references.

So if future me is reading this:

You were not lucky.
You worked.
You survived.
You adapted.
You are still building.

And don’t forget —
Stability makes you calm.
But growth makes you alive.
You need both.
