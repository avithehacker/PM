A Boy From Varanasi

(He writes because he forgets.)

Last Updated: March 2026
Age:

This is not for LinkedIn.
This is for reference.
Because I forget how things actually felt.

1. Where I Started

Varanasi

I was born on 4 December 1996 in Varanasi.

Varanasi is slow. Very slow. It teaches patience without saying it.

My father had a shop near BHU Trauma Center. I used to sit there sometimes. I saw people waiting outside the hospital. I saw worried faces. I saw families fully dependent on doctors.

I didn't understand much at that time. But I think I learned something silently:
Life can change anytime. Stability is not guaranteed.

Maybe that is why I want stability so badly today.

2. Class 10

The Stable Happiness

Sunbeam Academy, Varanasi.

This was the best time. We were not ambitious. We were busy.

School → Coaching → Cricket → PS2 → Repeat.

We had fixed friends. Fixed routine. Fixed life.

Now I understand something important:
We were happy because of pattern. Same people. Same system. No uncertainty.

Lesson to remember:
Stability makes me calm. Routine makes me happy.
3. Class 11 & 12

The Break

Everything changed. School changed. Friends changed. I got suspended. I moved to KV BHU.

Those two years were heavy. Board exams in India are not normal exams. They are generational pressure. My grandfather gave them. My father gave them. Then I gave them.

I saw parents emotionally dependent on children's marks. I didn't like that idea. I asked myself:
How can you depend fully on someone else for your happiness?

At the same time, I also felt pressure. I passed with good marks.

But the bigger lesson was this:
Dependency is risky.
Pressure builds you if you survive it.
I survived it.
4. College – BCA

University of Lucknow

College was better. More free. Less pressure. Only 11th and 12th were really hard phases in my life. Rest has been manageable.

Final year project: Prixfit
Idea: Farmers don't know where they get better crop prices. So build a system that shows mandi prices and helps them decide.

I still like that thinking. I always liked solving real problems.

What college gave me? Honestly, no powerful network. No strong reference. I never had big influential people around me. Everything I built, I built mostly alone.

Sometimes I feel proud of that.
Sometimes I feel maybe having a strong network could have made things faster.
But reality: I built without dependency.
5. Startup

2019 to 2022

After graduation, I started my own startup for visually impaired students. We wanted to make learning simple for them. Because books are not easily accessible. And accessibility in India is still weak.

Then COVID came. Money was low. Hope was low. Exchange program cancelled. No clarity about future.

We survived. Somehow.

What I actually built there
  • Spent more than 600 hours directly talking to visually impaired students — not surveys, real conversations, real pain.
  • Built an educational platform based on what they actually needed.
  • Created and led a team of 12 people.
  • Added 1000+ accessible content pieces — more than 7200 hours of usable learning material.
  • Switched from expensive licensing model to royalty-free — costs dropped by 90%. That decision saved us.
  • Partnered with schools and NGOs for reach, not branding.
  • Within 9 months, generated revenue. More than 5000 visually impaired individuals used the platform.

But growth was slow. Scale was unclear. At some point I realised it was becoming a job, not a scalable system.

Lesson: Dreams need distribution. Impact needs a business model.
6. Smartivity

Learning Business Reality (2022)

I joined Smartivity as Product Manager. Small company. But real revenue. That itself was a lesson.

What I built there
  • Led a team of six people — marketing, sales, customer success, agencies.
  • Rebuilt the website. Reduced friction. Conversion rate increased by 64%.
  • Improved pricing and retention plans. Orders increased by 83%.
  • Ran multiple A/B experiments. Browse-to-Add-to-Cart improved by 47%.
  • Added trust badges properly. Cart-to-purchase increased by 30%.
  • Improved website speed. Performance improved by 50%.

I learned funnels, CRO, and Amazon vs D2C trust psychology. But the bigger lesson was about people — some protect salary, some protect ego, few protect revenue.

Lesson: Revenue is good. Direction is better.
7. Current

Mahindra & Mahindra

Now I work at Mahindra as Product Manager. Bigger organisation. More stakeholders. More structure.

Earlier I built in chaos. Now I build inside a system. Both teach different muscles.

What I am building here
  • Built a seller platform from scratch — live auction tracking, price comparison, market data.
  • Pushed automation across the full auction lifecycle: listing → approval → payment. Manual work reduced heavily.
  • Led the full redesign of Ediig — Mobile, Desktop, Android, iOS.
  • Tested Open vs Close Auctions. Measured bidder behavior. Optimised for profit and engagement.
  • Implemented Redis caching — speed and stability improved significantly.
  • Automated PAN and Aadhaar verification via AuthBridge — KYC time dropped from 24–48 hours to 10 minutes.
  • Integrated SAP with the portal — manual reconciliation reduced by 80%.
  • Added invoicing inside the platform — 100% accuracy, no manual chaos.
  • Built multi-language support in the app — more accessibility, wider adoption.

Now I am learning scale, governance, and long-term system thinking.

8. Physical Reset

Discipline Works

Three years ago I was overweight.

Now I am around 82 kg. Target below 70.
I walk around 9 km daily.
I quit sugar.

This is not about looks. This is proof that I can redesign myself.

Discipline works.
9. Internal Struggles

Improving Slowly

  • I struggle with speaking in front of CEO-level people. Sometimes I just read instead of presenting confidently.
  • I struggle with writing clearly even when I know the idea.
  • I overthink sometimes.
  • I want stability too much.

But I am improving. Slowly.

10. My Ambition

(Which I Don't Say Loudly)

One day, I will build again.

But this time:
  • With better system.
  • With capital awareness.
  • With distribution strategy.
  • With long-term thinking.

I don't want to just manage features.
I want to build platforms.

11. Why I Am Writing This

Because I forget.

  • I forget how hard 11th was.
  • I forget how scary COVID felt.
  • I forget how proud I felt after losing weight.
  • I forget how much effort it took to reach here without big references.

So if future me is reading this:

You were not lucky.
You worked.
You survived.
You adapted.
You are still building.

And don't forget —
Stability makes you calm.
But growth makes you alive.
You need both.
